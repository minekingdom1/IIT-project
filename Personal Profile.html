Personal profile and online test results

What does the online Myers-Briggs test say about me?

From the Myers-Briggs test on the website 16Personalities, I was placed as a turbulent adventurer type. To be specific, my code was ISFP-T, my role was explorer and my strategy was constant improvement.
The website defines the adventurer type as people who "tend to have open minds, approaching life, new experiences, and people with grounded warmth." The website also states that "their ability to stay in the moment helps them uncover exciting potentials."
The T in the code ISFP-T is defines me as a turbulent individual, someone who is self-concious and sensitive to stress.

What about my learning style? What does an online learning style test say about me?

From the learning style test on the website EducationPlanner.org, I am a visual learner. They are defined as best learning through sight, usually neat and clean and closing their eyes to visualize or remember something.
If bored, the websited states that they "will find something to watch" and they may have difficulty with spoken directions and may be easily distracted by sounds.

How about a creativity test? What does an online creativity test say about me?

My creative score according to the website TestMyCreativity.com is a 54.94, lower than the typical 63. They state that the metrics they use were:

  • Abstraction which is the ability to abstract concepts from ideas
  • Connection which is the ability to make connections between things that don't initially have an apparent connection
  • Perspective or the ability to shift ones perspective on a situation - in terms of space and time, and other people
  • Curiosity or the desire to change or improve things that everyone else accepts as the norm
  • Boldness, the confidence to push boundaries beyond accepted conventions. Also the ability to eliminate fear of what others think of you
  • Paradox, the ability to simultaneously accept and work with statements that are contradictory
  • Complexity, the ability to carry large quantities of information and be able to manipulate and manage the relationships between such information
  • Persistence, the ability to force oneself to keep trying to derive more and stronger solutions even when good ones have already been generated

What does the rsults of these tests mean for me?

They mean very little to me. Typically, my emotions tend to dictate my actions more than my personality. I already knew that I am stress sensitive and a visual learner. I however did not know that I would be defined as an adventurer/explorer type. Though it was something that I did not expect, it still means very little to me what a website defines me as.

How do you think these results may influence your behaviour in a team?

It would indicate that I am best when I am at my most comfortable and while willing, if stress is involved such as time restrictions, I will not be excited about learning new skills.
I am also labelled as an adventurer, but I am not a leader. While I do have some leadership skills, I am still an introvert at heart and prefer assisting from the shadow rather than in the light.

How should you take this into accoudn when forming a team?

When forming a team, someone else would have to be the leader. I would also probably require someone like the leader to push or organise small deadlines for me as I tend to procrastinate a lot. Tl;dr I am good at assisting others but need pushing myself.
